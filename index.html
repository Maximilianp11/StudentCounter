Bananas

Where are Bananas?

Bananas are grown in 135 different countries around the world with the largest producers of bananas being China and India who together account for approximately 26% of global Banana production. Bananas are also a staple food in many of the tropical regions on earth, like how in Africa, plantains and bananas provide more than 25 percent of the caloric requirements for over 70 million people.

Types of Bananas

There are many different species of bananas, but they all fall into 1 of 2 categories, cooking bananas called plantains, and dessert bananas. Plantains are very starchy and are not meant to be eaten raw and are supposed to be cooked before eating. Plantains are a staple food in many of the tropical regions on earth and are used similarly to how we use potatoes in the US. Dessert bananas on the other hand are the bananas you would find at the grocery store and you can just eat them without having to cook them at all and are generally more flavorful than plantains. Bananas belonging to the cavendish group are the most widespread species of banana and dominate the global banana market.

Banana Facts

  • Bananas are a non-seasonal crop and are therefore available year round
  • Banana fruits develop from a banana heart which has around 9 tiers and each tier sprouts about 20 bananas, so a fully developed heart can weigh up to 143 lbs

  • Bananas are picked when they are green (unripe) and are transported to the desination country and are then ripened in special airtight rooms filled with ethylene gas which help induce ripening.
  • Bananas were orginally from Southeast Asia and were brought to South America and later Africa by european traders/explorers
  • A green banana can be placed in a paper bag with an apple or tomato to speed up the ripening process
